\section{Literature review}

[You may structure this as you wish. However, the main purpose of this section is to provide the reader with good insight into the knowledge and research landscape surrounding your project. This does not mean that you will have to undertake a detailed sweep of everything ever done that might relate in any way to the project - this would be impossible anyway! What it means is that you will have to make a selection of important and representative work, all obtained from reliable sources, cited within the text and listed in the references section of the document, that to the best of your knowledge paints a landscape true to reality.

As this is an applied project, the literature review should include both information on the domain of application and on technologies that will be employed. For example, for a project on the prediction of startup company success with the use of machine learning (ML) models, the domain review would focus on startup company success, how it is measured, whether it was predicted before, how it was predicted (ML, other methods, or both?) and how successfully. The technology section of the review would focus on the machine learning models employed, their success in tackling similar problems and their suitability, in technical terms, for the particular problem at hand.

The literature review must give a cohesive and coherent account of the application and methodological domains and must be fully backed up by reliable sources (meaning that any information in it must be accompanied by a citation, unless it is common knowledge or follows logically from the content).

The literature review should start from the more general and focus in on the more specific as it develops, ending in a subsection that identifies gaps in knowledge from which the need for your project arises, but without mentioning the project itself: for example, a need (1) to try a new or slightly modified method on an old problem, (2) to apply an established method to new data (e.g. Irish instead of UK data) or (3) to confirm some previously obtained results.

Detailed descriptions of methods or other information that is commonly known in the field of machine learning and AI, should not be included in the literature review (or elsewhere in the report). More obscure or newer methods may be described. A good rule of thumb is: if you have learnt it in one of your modules, then it should not be written-up as part of the report.

Include at least 8 references. These must be reliable sources i.e. peer reviewed literature or books. If there is not a lot of peer-reviewed work on your topic (which may be the case if what you are doing is very new and relies on new technologies), you may use ‘gray literature’ but this must be properly justified in the text of this section.

Citations should be in author-year style. For example, you might say that \textcite{Sedgwick2012} did something spectacular and that it was widely reported \parencite{Myatt2007}.]
